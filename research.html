Bio

I am an applied microeconomist with interests in (i) political economy, where I study how political institutions and incentives shape policy design and implementation; (ii) development and culture, where I study how informal institutions and norms shape behavior and the effectiveness of policy in developing-country settings; and (iii) gender, where I study the determinants of gender gaps in household decisions, and labor-market trajectories.

Across projects, I combine experimental and observational methods, including administrative data, text-as-data, and machine-learning tools.

I currently work on projects in India, Denmark, and the United Kingdom.

I will join Princeton University as a postdoctoral researcher in September 2026.
